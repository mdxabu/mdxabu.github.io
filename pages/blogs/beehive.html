Beehive

A Data Federation Approach to Analyze Behavioral Health and Supplement Healthcare Practice with Community Health Metrics in Alaska

Project Description

This project aims to analyze behavioral health and supplement healthcare practice with community health metrics in Alaska using a data federation approach. By leveraging data from various sources, we can gain insights into behavioral health patterns and improve healthcare practices in the community.

Detailed Explanation about Beehive

This project is a collaborative effort between the University of Alaska Anchorage's Departments of Computer Science and Human Services. Its goal is to create a digital system that translates and stores art and photographic images in a database. This database is intended to support various human services and health care initiatives, particularly for individuals who are vulnerable and marginalized.

Key Aspects of the Project

  1. Objective:
    • Digital Repository Creation: Develop a digital repository to store images in formats that are easily retrievable and usable.
    • Support Vulnerable Populations: Use this repository to enhance the delivery of services to people who are highly vulnerable or marginalized within the community.
  2. Focus on Outsider Art:
    • Outsider Art: The repository will include images of Outsider Art. Outsider Art is created by individuals who are not formally trained as artists and often face significant discrimination.
    • Purpose: This focus helps to bring attention to the artistic expressions of these individuals, who may be underrepresented in traditional art institutions.
  3. Applications of the Repository:
    • Research: Facilitate research into Outsider Art and Outsider Artists. This can provide insights into the unique perspectives and experiences of these creators.
    • Education: Educate health and human services practitioners about the impact of negative stereotypes and discrimination on the health and well-being of vulnerable populations.
    • Arts Programs: Support arts programs aimed at improving the health and well-being of vulnerable individuals. By showcasing their art, the project aims to foster a greater understanding and appreciation of their experiences.

Benefits

  • Enhanced Visibility: Provides a platform for Outsider Artists, offering them visibility and recognition that they might not otherwise receive.
  • Informed Practitioners: Helps practitioners better understand the needs and experiences of marginalized individuals, leading to more empathetic and effective service delivery.
  • Support for Arts Initiatives: Promotes the use of art as a tool for health and well-being, potentially leading to more supportive and therapeutic arts programs.
